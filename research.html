Research

My research interests are diverse, but mostly related to diversity, evolution and ecology of invertebrates. One of my early interests was to understand how ecosystems work, how animal species interact with each other, how species are distributed in space and why. Currently I am working on metabarcoding of insects, focused on method optimization. The aim is to help ecologists in answering the former questions by having much more of information than with traditional surveys.

Polyclad flatworms were an important part of my research as well, and I still dedicate part of my time to them, collaborating in the distance with the team at MNCN in Madrid. In the past I also worked with ecology of meiofauna, studying how several factors of the environment can affect the distribution of the different taxonomic groups that form part of this community.

Finally, there are some topics that fascinate me, but I haven't had the opportunity or time to work with them, although I always try to learn more about. Those include:

  • Evolution. Especially adaptation, evolutionary innovations, response to selective pressure.
  • Understudied invertebrate groups. Kinorryncha, Tardigrada, "Turbellaria", Xenacoelomorpha, "Gnathifera", Chaetognatha.
  • Early Bilateria evolution and phylogeny of Spiralia.
  • The Ctenophora-sister/Porifera-sister conundrum.
  • Deep sea.

More details about my research:


Metabarcoding

Primer design, marker choice and lab methods testing for improving the protocol for metabarcoding of insects in bulk samples and eDNA. Use of metabarcoding data to explore the phylogeography of selected groups.

Polycladida

Taxonomy and systematics of polyclad flatworms and its diversity in the Iberian Peninsula and the Great Barrier Reef. Eco-evolutive implications of the colouration pattern and other morphological caracters.

Meiofauna

Changes in comunity composition of meiofauna due to depth and sediment granulometry. Meiofauna as bioindicator for pollutioun using a Mediterranean harbour - nature reserve system as a case-study.
